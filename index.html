A statistical accounting2006–2026

The gunfire, counted to the block.

Every shooting the police logged in two decades — 24,127 of them — placed, dated and tallied. The arc is not one story but several: a long decline, a pandemic shock, a recovery. The question this page keeps testing is how much actually changed, and where.

Most shootings hit one person — but not all

Methodology, sources & limitations

Sources. NYC Open Data's shooting collection (published Feb 2026): Shootings (5ucz-vwe8), Victims (pztn-9bne), Offenders (gdk4-mbsv), and the shoreline-clipped Police Precincts (y76i-bdw7). Records run ; 2026 is partial.

Counting. "Shootings" are incidents (one row per event); victims/offenders join on incident_key. An incident is fatal if any victim carries the statistical-murder flag. The source latitude/longitude columns are swapped; we correct them before mapping. Junk ages (e.g. 1022) and mixed sex labels are normalized.

Neighborhoods & change windows. The geographic unit is the NYPD precinct, labeled with the neighborhood it mainly covers (approximate, for readability). Share of city total compares a pre-pandemic window (2015–2019 average) with a recent one (2021–2025). Pandemic spikes compare 2018–2019 with 2020–2021. Declines compare 2020–2021 with 2023–2025. Low-volume precincts (under 60 lifetime shootings) are excluded from the change tables for stability. Counts are raw, not population-adjusted — read as "where," not personal risk.

Confidence. High for incident counts, geography, timing and victim demographics; medium for offender demographics (identified-suspect subset) and the demographic-share trends (category definitions can shift over twenty years). Nothing is modeled — every figure is a direct count of published records.

How this was made. The pipeline, maps and charts were built with AI assistance (Claude). Every number is computed directly from the four datasets by a transparent script — nothing is AI-estimated. Checks: incident/victim/offender counts reconciled with source totals (24,127 / 28,912 / 18,901); the lat/lon swap caught and corrected against sample records and the precinct map; yearly totals and the fatal-incident join spot-checked; precinct change windows hand-verified for several neighborhoods. Not independently re-reported: NYPD classification, geocoding and arrest decisions, taken as published.
Source: NYPD via NYC Open Data · an independent civic-data project, not affiliated with the NYPD or the City of New York.
